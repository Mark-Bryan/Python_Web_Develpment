OAR. Last weekend, the American circuit, configured for the 24 Hours at Daytona with a 3.56-mile (5.7 km) layout, hosted the first seven free practice sessions during the ROAR Before the 24, which were partially affected by heavy rain. The best lap time for a Ferrari in GTD Pro was set by Costa in the DragonSpeed 296 GT3, placing eighth overall with a time of 1:48.263; ninth in the combined standings was teammate Molina (1:48.278). In GTD, the top Ferrari car was AF Corse’s number 21, third for Italian driver Pier Guidi (1:48.131), who also recorded the best overall time for the Ferraris. History. Over the decades, the race has predominantly been contested over 24 hours, except between 1962 and 1966, and in 1972, when it featured different durations. During this time, the Maranello manufacturer has claimed 17 class victories and five overall wins. The most recent class victory was in 2024, when official drivers Davide Rigon, Daniel Serra, Alessandro Pier Guidi, and James Calado triumphed in GTD Pro with Risi Competizione’s 296 GT3 after 733 laps. In the annals of Ferrari history and the minds of its tifosi, the 1967 victory stands out, with Lorenzo Bandini and Chris Amon triumphing at the wheel of the F330 P3/P4. The race ended with a parade finish for Ferrari, with the second and third spots taken by the 330 P4 of Mike Parkes and Ludovico Scarfiotti and the 412 P of Pedro Rodriguez and Jean Guichet, respectively. The programme. On Thursday, 23 January, two free practice sessions for all classes are scheduled from 10.05 to 11.35 a.m. and from 6.30 to 8.00 p.m., followed by qualifying: from 2.10 to 2.25 p.m. for the GTD class and from 2.35 to 2.50 p.m. for the GTD Pro class. On Friday, 24, crews return on the track for a third free practice session from 11.20 a.m. to 12.20 p.m. The 24 Hours at Daytona will start on Saturday, 25 January, at 1.40 p.m. and finish at the same time the following day (all times are local).
